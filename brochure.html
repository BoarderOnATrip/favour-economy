Problem One

The Peeping Tom
in Your Pocket

Every app you open, every search you make, every link you click, every pause before a scroll — it is all watched, recorded, sold, and weaponised against you. Not metaphorically. Literally.

They call it a "free service."
What it is: a surveillance apparatus that harvests your most intimate data and sells it to the highest bidder — without your meaningful consent.

👁️
Location Tracking
They know where you sleep.
Your phone's GPS is sold to data brokers who build a profile of every location you have visited — home, doctor, church, protest, bedroom.
🎙️
Ambient Listening
They hear what you don't post.
Microphone access granted once — used indefinitely. Conversations, background noise, and emotional tone all feed the behavioural model.
🧠
Psychological Profiling
They model your mind.
Scroll patterns, pause duration, reread behaviour, and content dwell time build a psychological model more accurate than a therapist's — used to manipulate, not help.
💳
Financial Surveillance
They know before you spend.
Purchase intent signals are harvested before the transaction. Advertisers bid on your "intent" in real time — before you have consciously decided.
👥
Social Graph Mapping
They map every relationship.
Everyone you know, how close you are, who influences you, who you trust — all mapped, all sold, all used to target you through the people you love.
🔓
Zero Consent
You agreed in paragraph 47.
Terms of service are written by lawyers, for lawyers. The consent model is designed to fail you. The product is your compliance — not your wellbeing.
The Peeping Tom Principle
A stranger standing outside your window watching your every move would be arrested. The same behaviour — recording your location, listening to your conversations, watching who you talk to, tracking your emotional state — is legal, profitable, and celebrated as long as it is done through an app you downloaded.

The only difference is the interface. The violation is identical.

Problem Two

You Are the Product.
Consumerism Is the Unit.

The internet was supposed to liberate you. Instead it engineered you into the most compliant consumer in history — a passive node that absorbs ads, generates data, and confuses spending with living.

The Consumerism Funnel — You go in at the top. Value leaves at the bottom.

Every scroll, every notification, every "limited time offer" is precision-engineered to convert your attention into someone else's revenue.
📱
Engagement Maximisation
Addictive by design.
Platform algorithms are optimised for one metric: time on app. Outrage, anxiety, and envy are the most effective engagement drivers — and they know it. Your mental health is an acceptable casualty.
🛒
Manufactured Desire
You didn't want it. Then you did.
Retargeting, social proof, scarcity signals, and influencer networks are deployed to create desire for things you did not previously want, using your own data to find the psychological lever.
🔄
The Subscription Trap
Passive drain, forever.
Recurring charges designed to be forgotten. Cancellation designed to be painful. The business model is your inertia — and billions of dollars depend on you not noticing the charge.
💸
Debt as a Feature
Spend now, pay forever.
Buy-now-pay-later, credit normalisation, and compound interest are built into the consumer layer. The economy depends on you spending money you don't have. Debt is not an accident. It is the product.
