01 · Campaign Brief

One world. Three doors in.

The Unfolding is the hero spot — sixty seconds, big idea, cinema. The Trilogy are the three doors that follow it: shorter films, each chosen so a different person can find themselves in the frame. The mother who fears her phone. The maker who is tired of being mined. The neighbor who remembers what trust used to feel like. Same world. Same palette. Same single cello note. A different way in.

The hero spot is the cathedral. The Trilogy is the three side-chapels — each lit by the same candle, each with a different saint at the altar.

Each spot is shootable as a standalone, deployable in a single channel, and ladders directly back to the hero film through a shared visual rhyme: the gold paper. In The Whisper it folds into a bird. In The Maker it is a coin. In The Table it is a ledger. The audience does not need to be told these are the same paper — their eye will know.

02 · The Paper · Across the Trilogy

One sheet, three forms.

Every spot ends with the same square of gold paper folded into a different object. The fold is always done by an unseen hand — a quiet gesture, no SFX, a single typewriter tick as the form completes. This is the brand mnemonic. If the audience sees gold paper folding in any context for the next decade, they will think of the Favour Economy.

Spot 01 · Privacy
The Crane
A bird made from the paper. It sits on the windowsill at dawn. The mother's silence has wings.
Spot 02 · Producer
The Token
A coin folded from the paper — the FavourBuck. Pressed into the baker's flour-white hand.
Spot 03 · Group
The Ledger
A book folded from the paper. Twelve names inside. Owned by no one, kept by everyone.

03 · Spot One · Privacy

The Whisper.

The mother spot. The one that lands on the school-pickup audience, the one we put on YouTube preroll before parenting and home-improvement videos. Built around the most universal modern fear — that the device on the kitchen counter is not actually on your side. It does not solve the fear. It names it, and offers a place to stand.

04 · Spot Two · Producer

The Maker.

The dignity-of-labour spot. The one that runs in the cinema before Nomadland-shaped films, on long-form podcast preroll, and on the homepage of every co-op, farmers market, and trade school in the country. It is the rebuttal to "you are the product." It is the line: you are the producer, said quietly, while you watch a woman do real work in the dark hours.

07 · Channel · Placement · Cut

Where each spot lives.

Each spot has been designed for a specific surface. Cinema rewards stillness; broadcast rewards intimacy; preroll rewards a hook in the first three seconds; social rewards a gestural payoff with no audio. The 30s cuts are the masters; the 15s and 6s cuts are derived, not re-shot.

08 · Release Timeline · 12 Weeks

The waterfall.

The hero spot lights the room. The trilogy keeps the room lit. Releases are spaced so each spot has air to breathe — and so each subsequent spot lands on an audience already primed by the last. By week 12, all three trilogy spots and the hero film are running concurrently across the appropriate surfaces.

09 · Production Budget Rollup

Three films. One shoot week.

All three trilogy spots can be produced in a single week if cast and crew double up across spots. The Whisper and The Maker share a baker (different role), the same cinematographer, the same colorist. The Table is the only one requiring a second location and a larger ensemble. Numbers below assume a producer-friendly tier — push higher for cinema-finish, lower for indie-edit-room.
