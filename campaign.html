01 · Campaign Brief

One world. Four doors in.

The Favour Economy Protocol Whitepaper defines every piece of the machine — the Logistic, the Surface, the Rider, the Witness layer, the Group, the FavourBuck, the Mesh. But the whitepaper also says this: "Every protocol needs a creation story. Ours is a bag of milk, hand-delivered, with a note." That creation story is Spot Zero — the sixty-second prologue that grounds everything that follows.

The Unfolding is the hero spot — big idea, cinema. Spot Zero is the origin story that precedes it. The Trilogy are the three doors that follow: shorter films, each chosen so a different person can find themselves in the frame. The mother who fears her phone. The maker whose Surface — her time, her skill, her bread — is extracted by platforms. The neighbours who remember what a Group used to feel like. Same world. Same palette. Same single cello note. A different way in.

The origin story is the seed. The hero spot is the cathedral. The Trilogy is the three side-chapels — each lit by the same candle, each with a different saint at the altar.

Each spot is shootable as a standalone, deployable in a single channel, and ladders directly back to the origin film through a shared visual rhyme: the gold paper. In Spot Zero it is a handwritten note inside a cooler. In The Whisper it folds into a bird. In The Maker it is a coin — the FavourBuck. In The Table it is a ledger — the Witness record. The audience does not need to be told these are the same paper — their eye will know.

02 · The Paper · Across the Trilogy

One sheet, four forms.

Every spot ends with the same square of gold paper transformed into a different object. The fold is always done by an unseen hand — a quiet gesture, no SFX, a single typewriter tick as the form completes. This is the brand mnemonic. If the audience sees gold paper folding in any context for the next decade, they will think of the Favour Economy. The whitepaper calls this moment "what the protocol does not see" — the social residue that remains between the parties.

03 · Spot Zero · Origin

The Bag of Milk.

The creation story. Direct from §5.1 of the whitepaper — "The breastmilk, told straight." This is the sixty-second film that plays before everything else. Before privacy. Before production. Before group. Before the protocol. Before the paper folds. A bag of frozen milk, hand-delivered with a note, between two mothers who have never met. The Logistic that started it all — an Origin, a Destination, a Time constraint, and a Rider specifying: breastmilk, transportability, willingness, trust.

04 · Spot One · Privacy

The Whisper.

The mother spot. The one that lands on the school-pickup audience, the one we put on YouTube preroll before parenting and home-improvement videos. Built around the most universal modern fear — that the device on the kitchen counter is not actually on your side. In the whitepaper's language: the old economy treats your voice, your location, your sleep as a Surface it can harvest without consent. The Favour Economy inverts this — your Surface is yours to declare, on your terms.

05 · Spot Two · Producer

The Maker.

The dignity-of-labour spot. The one that runs in the cinema before Nomadland-shaped films, on long-form podcast preroll, and on the homepage of every co-op, farmers market, and trade school in the country. In protocol terms: the baker's time, her skill, her oven, her bread — these are her Surface. The old economy extracts a platform cut from every Logistic she fulfils. The Favour Economy routes the FavourBuck directly — 98% to her, 1% to her Group, 1% to the system. The box is hers.

06 · Spot Three · Group

The Table.

The community spot. The slowest of the three. The one we put on Sunday-morning broadcast and at the head of the trail in every cooperative, faith community, and mutual-aid group's video library. In protocol terms: a Group — "a collective of members who elect to pool a defined percentage of their transaction flow into a shared fund, used to acquire shared assets, underwrite mutual insurance, pay dividends, and fund group favours that exceed any individual's capacity." The barn they raise is a Logistic no one could fulfil alone. The ledger is the Witness layer — the record that nobody owns and everybody keeps.

09 · Release Timeline · 12 Weeks

The waterfall.

The hero spot lights the room. The trilogy keeps the room lit. Releases are spaced so each spot has air to breathe — and so each subsequent spot lands on an audience already primed by the last. By week 12, all three trilogy spots and the hero film are running concurrently across the appropriate surfaces.
