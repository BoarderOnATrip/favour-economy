Cinematic Trailer

FAVOUR ECONOMY

The Revolution — Full Shot Script

Duration: ~2:28  ·  Tone: Apple × Inception × Black Mirror Optimism  ·  Colour: Black → Gold → White

1
The Void — 4s
Pure black screen. Complete silence. Four full seconds.
Sound: Absolute silence — then one low sub-bass note, felt more than heard. A single piano key.
Nothing. The audience leans in.
2
The Need — 3s
Extreme close-up. A hand reaching for a phone, screen glow reflected on skin. Night. Somewhere urban. Anywhere on Earth. The hand could be anyone's.
Sound: Sub-bass sustains. One piano key. No reverb. Intimate.
"Every day..."
3
The Scale — 4s
Pull back — not just one hand — we see a continent from satellite altitude. Millions of light-points, human activity at 2am. It breathes.
Sound: Strings enter, low. The scale of the sound matches the scale of the image.
"8 billion people need something."
4
The Mirror — 4s
CUT TO BLACK. Hard cut. One beat. Then: the same continent, same lights — but the lights pulse slightly differently. These are the givers, not the takers. Identical. Separated by nothing but the system.
Sound: Same strings. A breath. The gap between two notes.
"8 billion people have something to offer."
5
The Gap — 5s
Two identical city grids — slide in from left and right — separated by a thin dark line. The line slowly widens into an abyss. The grids strain toward each other.
Sound: Tension. A second low drone enters beneath the strings.
"The gap between them... has always been the system."
6
The Logistic — 5s
CUT TO BLACK. Hard silence, one full second. Then — a single white dot appears on pure black. It moves. A second dot appears. A golden line draws itself between them, slowly, with intention.
Sound: The moment the line appears — a clean, high piano tone. Sustain. The sound of connection.
THE LOGISTIC
Gold text. Full screen. Nothing else.
7
Logistic Anatomy — 6s
Minimalist animation — city grid from above, pure dark. Copper dot (pickup) appears first. Gold dot (destination) second. A time arc — like a countdown — forms above the connecting path.
Sound: Music builds. Percussion enters. Each element's appearance gets its own soft click.
[ ORIGIN ] — a location. / [ DESTINATION ] — another. / [ TIME ] — a constraint. / [ THE RYDER ] — a human who connects them.
8
The Ryder — 5s
Close-up montage — each 1.5 seconds: a cyclist weaving morning traffic; a woman loading a car efficiently; a man on subway reading who looks up at a notification; a teenager on a scooter taking a corner. They all say yes.
Sound: Warm percussion. The cuts have rhythm. Human scale.
"Ordinary people. Extraordinary reach."
9
Fast or Free — 6s
Split screen — left half bleeds copper, city at speed; right half glows gold, city at ease. FAST on the left. FREE on the right. A divider line that shimmers between them.
Sound: The music drops to near-silence. The space between FAST and FREE is a beat of silence. Then returns doubled in intensity.
"The network decides. You choose."
10
Network Optimizing — 6s
God's-eye view of a city at night. Ryders appear as moving gold points. Logistics pop up as copper lines — match — resolve — pulse green. The network is alive, routing, breathing, solving.
Sound: Full crescendo. This is the emotional peak of act two. Fast, hopeful, vast.
"Free time. Capital. Equipment. Labour. Services. The network sees what you cannot."
11
FavourBucks — 5s
Abstract. Geometrical fragments of light — not coins, but something richer. Origami folds in slow motion. Encryption spiraling like DNA. Value that doesn't decay.
Sound: Lighter, musical. Almost playful. But still vast.
"Every favour earned is a favour remembered. Every favour remembered grows the system."
12
The Mesh — 7s
The most cinematic shot. A globe — dark, turning slowly. It's covered in tiny lights, like neural tissue. The lights pulse in waves. The mesh breathes. It expands, reaches deserts, mountains, islands, oceans.
Sound: Quiet. Almost sacred. A choir note, held.
"1% of every favour... builds the internet that no one owns."
13
Pay to Map — 6s
First-person: a person moving through a Lagos market — their phone captures and maps. Cut: a village in Peru, same motion. Cut: flood zone in Bangladesh. The 3D mesh builds behind each of them in real time. They are building the world's most valuable map.
Sound: Warmer now. Human scale. The music has become something you could hum.
"Pay people to map the world. Let the world own the map."
14
Insurance / Welfare — 7s
Three images, warm light, 2.5s each: a newborn's hand curled around an adult finger; an elderly woman laughing in a circle of people; a child running toward the camera, free.
Sound: Full. Warm. The emotional resolution of everything built before.
"Life insurance. Child insurance. Elderly care. Not a charity. The natural output of a system that works."
15
The Turn — 2s
CUT TO BLACK. Hard. Complete. Two full seconds.
Sound: SILENCE. Then one deep bass thud — felt in the chest. Nothing else.
Nothing.
The audience is quiet. They wait.
16
Not an Evolution — 4s
Slow zoom out from a single human face — looking up. Calm. Certain. The world behind them lit gold.
Sound: The full orchestra enters. Strings + brass + electronic bass. This is the crescendo. One word at a time.
"This isn't an evolution."
17
The Revolution — 4s
WHITE FLASH — 0.3 seconds, pure white, full bleed. Then: the globe, fully illuminated. The mesh covers it completely. Every Logistic flowing. Every favour completing. Every person fed.
Sound: FULL. Every instrument. The moment the audience has been waiting for.
IT'S A REVOLUTION.
18
Logo Reveal — 5s
Fade to deep black. The logo appears — slowly, piece by piece. Minimal. Geometric. A rotating diamond form, gold on black. The mark of a new world.
Sound: Fades back to the single sub-bass note from shot 1. Full circle. The beginning is the end.
FAVOUR ECONOMY · "Every person. Every favour. Every future."
19
CTA — 4s
Same black. Minimal. No fanfare. Just the domain. An invitation.
Sound: Near silence. The sub-bass note fades to nothing.
favoureconomy.com — Join the network. →
Total runtime: ~2:28

Production Notes

Score direction: Hans Zimmer × Brian Eno. Starts sub-20Hz sub-bass. Builds through strings, then brass, then full electronic synthesis. The silence at shot 15 must be absolute. The white flash at shot 17 must be bright enough to feel physical. Colour grade: deep teal-black shadows, gold highlights, copper mid-tones. No cool-blue grading.
