Getting Started Guide

Five minutes to
your first FavourBuck.

This guide walks you through your first Logistic, builds your Surface, finds your Group, and optionally pairs your desktop. Each step earns you more.

1 Step One

Post your first Logistic.

A Logistic is the fundamental unit of the Favour Economy. It's a pickup, a dropoff, and a time constraint. Attach a Rider — your spec sheet — and the network finds someone to fulfil it.

Origin
Where is the pickup?
Any location — home, office, store, curb. Be as specific as you need. The network matches Ryders within range.
Example: "Unit 4, 121 King St W, Toronto"
Destination
Where does it need to go?
Another address, a person's name, a drop zone. The Ryder delivers end-to-end. You get confirmation when it's done.
Example: "Sarah Chen, 88 Queen East, Suite 12"
Time Constraint
When does it need to arrive?
Set a window. Wider window = more Ryders = faster match. The network routes optimally within your constraint.
Example: "Before 5pm today" or "Anytime this week"
The Rider
What does the Ryder need?
The Rider is your spec sheet. Attach skills required, equipment needed, access codes, handling instructions. The network matches on fit.
Example: "Fragile. Needs car. Call on arrival."

4 Step Four — Optional

Pair your Desktop Bridge.

The Desktop Bridge turns your Mac or PC into a fully controllable node. Voice-command your computer from your phone. Run Logistics in the background while you work. Optional, but powerful.

On your phone

Go to Settings → Desktop Bridge → Add New Computer

  1. Tap Scan QR Code
  2. Point at your computer screen
  3. Bridge pairs automatically over your local network
  4. Test: say "take a screenshot" — your desktop responds

On your computer

The bridge server generates a pairing token. Display it from Settings → Bridge → Show Token, or via the terminal:

Bridge Token (example)
aB3k-9mPz-Xq7r-YvN2

Token rotates every 30 days. LAN-only by default. Never exposed to the internet unless you enable LAN mode.
