00 · Key Art

The poster.

The hero frame — the moment the gold paper meets dark water and decides, against physics, to fold itself. This is the image the campaign hinges on. It runs as the OOH print, the YouTube thumbnail, the LinkedIn header, the cinema slide before the lights drop.

01 · Logline

The story in one breath.

In a world that watches everything, a single sheet of gold paper falls into dark water — and chooses, against the current, to fold itself into a village.

02 · Emotional Arc

The dramatic shape.

A Pixar story is a curve, not a line. Dread climbs for eighteen seconds — long enough to be uncomfortable, short enough that the audience doesn't escape. The break at 0:18 is the entire film. Everything before it is the cage; everything after is the door.

03 · Color Script

The palette tells the story before the words do.

Pixar's Sharon Calahan does this for every film. Read the strip left to right: blood reds and surveillance blues collapse into a single sheet of gold, then bloom into wheat, candle, and finally a clean black with one gold word. The viewer's nervous system follows the colour even with the sound off.

Twelve panels.
The whole film.

Each panel is a complete idea — a frame an editor could cut to and the story would still survive. Read them in order; the rhythm is on the page. Aspect ratio is 2.39:1 (anamorphic) — every frame composed for cinema first, then crop-protected for square social.

05 · Shot List

The cinematographer's sheet.

Hand this directly to your DP. Every shot has a defined lens, movement, and lighting principle. The two halves of the film use different glass on purpose — the surveillance act is shot on cold modern primes (Zeiss Supreme), the favour act on warm vintage anamorphics (Cooke Anamorphic /i SF) for the lens flare and skin-tone falloff that pure digital can't fake.

06 · Sound Design

The score is restraint.

One cello carries Act I. The choir builds across Act II — voices added one at a time, never all at once. There is no drum hit, no whoosh, no stinger. The single most important moment is the silence between 0:18 and 0:20 — when the server-wall image holds and the audience is, for two full seconds, alone with what they have just been told.

07 · The Visual Through-Line

A single sheet of paper.

Pixar films are recognisable from a single frame. Yours should be too. The origami fold is the visual signature — it appears in five forms across the film, and once across every single piece of marketing the brand ever produces. Print, web, app icon, packaging, pitch deck. Same fold. Five stages.

08 · World Bible

Two worlds, one cut between them.

Pixar's Inside Out has Headquarters and Long Term Memory. Yours has the Surveillance World and the Favour World. Every department — DP, costume, score, sound, motion design — works from this single page. If a frame breaks the rule, it gets cut.

World A · Antagonist
The Surveillance World
Palette
Blood red, server-rack blue, phosphor green, charcoal black
Light
Cold practicals only — phone screens, LEDs, monitors. No sun.
Camera
Locked off, surveillance grammar — POV through devices, crosshair frames
Lens
Modern Zeiss Supreme primes — clinical, no flare, no soul
Texture
Plastic, glass, metal. No skin in close-up.
Motion
Either utterly still or jittered (handheld phone)
Type
JetBrains Mono — surveillance UI overlays, REC indicators, GPS coords
Sound
One cello. Server hum. UI shutter. The hum of a fridge in an empty house.
Bodies
Silhouettes only. Never a face. The audience never identifies with this world.
World B · Hero
The Favour World
Palette
Brushed gold, candle copper, wheat, oat, soft black
Light
Natural sun, porch lamps, kitchen tungsten. Magic hour preferred.
Camera
Documentary-warm — slight handheld breathing, never showy
Lens
Cooke Anamorphic /i SF — vintage falloff, gentle flare on practicals
Texture
Paper, wood, hands, wool, soil, water. Things that age well.
Motion
Slow-mo on humans (60fps). Time-lapse on infrastructure.
Type
Inter Light, generous letter-spacing — letters as objects, not labels
Sound
Choir building voice by voice. Wind. Distant bell. A single "thank you."
Bodies
Real people, real hands, no models. The audience IS this world.

Three doors. Pick one.
